Environmental Health Channel
Overview Visualization

The Environmental Health Channel is an interactive web-based platform for creating and sharing environmental sensing and health data narratives. This data, shared by affected residents and collected by EHP, includes physical and psychosocial health symptoms, particulate pollution (PM2.5) air measurements, and personal stories from residents. This tool displays this data using visualization and exploratory data analysis techniques. This enables researchers, health professionals, and the public to interactively explore and share compelling scientific evidence of local impacts of oil and gas drilling.

Public health scientists, medical professionals, and community service experts at the Environmental Health Project (EHP) are dedicated to assisting residents who believe they are, or could be affected by oil and gas drilling activities. Since 2014, EHP has collected highly-detailed health data via self-reporting surveys as well as environmental exposure information.
